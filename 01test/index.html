Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the ability to navigate digital environments safely, responsibly, and respectfully. It encompasses the skills and knowledge required to participate effectively in a digital society while upholding ethical, legal, and social norms.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the electronic responsibility for actions and deeds performed online. As a core element of digital citizenship, it focuses on the creation of rules, policies, and ethical standards that govern behavior in the digital world.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is a set of rules and guidelines that define how an organization's network, computer systems, and digital resources should be used. It functions as a "digital code of conduct" to ensure users act responsibly, legally, and safely.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's language, ideas, or original work and presenting it as your own without proper credit. While primarily an ethical offense related to academic integrity, it can become a legal issue if it involves copyright infringement or breach of contract.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any form of behavior that results in a student giving or receiving unauthorized assistance or receiving credit for work that is not their own. As of 2026, educational institutions have expanded these definitions to specifically address the rapid integration of artificial intelligence into academic workflows.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

In 2026, the primary rules for AI-generated content focus on transparency and accountability. Under new laws like the EU AI Act and the California AI Transparency Act, creators must clearly label synthetic media (images, video, and audio) and developers must disclose their training data sources. In academic and professional settings, AI cannot be credited as an author, and its use must be disclosed to ensure it does not bypass standards for human originality and ethical integrity.
